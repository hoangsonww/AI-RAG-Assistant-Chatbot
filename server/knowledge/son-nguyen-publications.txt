From Prompt Engineering to Intent Engineering: A Three-Layer Framework for Context Management in Production Agentic AI Systems
- Organization: University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, Department of Computer Science
- Date: March 2026
- Summary: Introduces the Context-Compound-Intent (CCI) Stack, a three-layer framework for managing context in production agentic AI systems. The framework decomposes prompt engineering into context engineering, compound orchestration, and intent alignment. Validated across five open-source systems, the study demonstrates progressive dependencies between the three layers and shows that ungated compounding degrades system performance. This work bridges the gap between ad-hoc prompt design and systematic, production-grade context management for autonomous AI agents.
- Link: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/402999775

Optimizing Web Performance and Computational Efficiency: A Deep Dive into WebAssembly's Technical Advancements and Real-World Applications
- Organization: University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, Department of Computer Science
- Date: April 2025
- Summary: An evaluation of recent WebAssembly advances that combines technical analysis with practical case studies. The paper examines how modern WASM capabilities — including improved compilation pipelines, SIMD support, and emerging component model proposals — improve runtime performance and computational efficiency in production web applications. Benchmarks and real-world case studies demonstrate measurable performance gains across compute-intensive workloads.
- Link: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/389517325

An AI-Driven, Cloud-Native Architecture for End-to-End Travel Planning: A System Design Study
- Organization: University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, Department of Computer Science
- Date: July 2025
- Summary: A cloud-native architecture study for an AI-powered travel agent built on Google Cloud Platform. The system leverages Vertex AI for intelligent planning, Dialogflow CX for natural language interaction, and Pub/Sub for event-driven communication. Stateless microservices handle itinerary generation, booking orchestration, and real-time disruption management. The paper explores micro-agent orchestration patterns and scalable GCP integration strategies for production travel planning systems.
- Link: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/392525972

Enhancing the Robustness of Transformer-Based Code Generation Models Against Adversarial Perturbations
- Organization: University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, Department of Computer Science
- Date: March 2025
- Summary: A comprehensive study of adversarial vulnerabilities in transformer-based code generation models. The paper provides a comparative review of hardening techniques — including adversarial training, input sanitization, and robust fine-tuning — aimed at improving the resilience, reliability, and security of AI-generated code. The findings highlight critical attack surfaces and propose defense strategies for production deployment of code generation systems.
- Link: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/389517109

A Comprehensive Analysis of Assisted Suicide: The Technical, Ethical, and Moral Dimensions
- Organization: University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, Department of Computer Science
- Date: December 2024
- Summary: A cross-disciplinary analysis of assisted suicide that compares physician-assisted and non-physician-assisted implementation methods. The study synthesizes medical, philosophical, and legal perspectives to provide a balanced evaluation of this complex issue.
